Castro Street

Castro Street gives the neighborhood its name. Its commercial heart sits around the 400–500 blocks; south of 20th Street it turns residential, lined with Victorian and Edwardian flats and houses.

700 block

Eleven buildings documented, all residential, built between 1895 and 1980. The two sides of the block sit differently in the historic record: the odd side falls inside a California Register historic district, the even side lies outside any district.

Odd side

  • 707 Castro Street
    A 1980 three-unit building — the newest on the block by some eighty years — standing inside the historic district.
  • 711–715 Castro Street
    A 1900 three-unit building on one parcel spanning three street numbers, with a permit record dominated by dry rot.
  • 737 Castro Street
    A 1900 house remodelled 2005–2007 for $270,000, including three new dormers and an attic conversion.

Even side

  • 700 Castro Street
    A 12-unit apartment building of 1927, seismically retrofitted in 2016 under the city's mandatory soft-story program.
  • 720 Castro Street
    A 1901 house sold in December 2024, the most recent transfer documented here.
  • 732 Castro Street
    A 1901 house of eleven rooms with a 1990 horizontal addition, and the lowest assessed value on the block.
  • 740 Castro Street
    A 1900 house identified as a historic resource, with $620,000 of permitted work between 2019 and 2021.
  • 742 Castro Street
    An 1895 four-unit building with the block's deepest permit record, running from 1978 to 2025.
  • 744 Castro Street
    An 1896 two-flat Victorian with a four-decade permit record.
  • 746 Castro Street
    An 1896 historic resource with a $750,000 remodel application under review.
  • 748 Castro Street
    A 1900 three-unit building, in the same ownership since 1987.

Historic district

The odd-numbered side of the block falls inside the Castro & Liberty Streets Historic District, so every parcel there carries CEQA Category A. The even side lies outside any district.
