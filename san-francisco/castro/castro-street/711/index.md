# 711–715 Castro Street

A three-unit, three-storey building of **1900** occupying one parcel across
three street numbers — 711, 713 and 715 — on the odd side of the 700 block,
which lies within the **Castro & Liberty Streets Historic District**. At 5,280
square feet it is the largest building documented on the block after the
apartment house at 700. Parcel 3603-073.

## One building, three addresses

The assessor records a single parcel (3603-073) whose location spans 711 to
715 Castro Street, and the Department of Building Inspection files the same
permit numbers under all three street numbers. This page covers the building
as a whole, at its lowest street number.

## The building

- Built 1900 (assessor roll)
- Three residential units ("Flats & Duplex" property class), 16 rooms,
  3 stories
- Building area 5,280 sq ft; lot 2,622 sq ft
- Zoning RH-3; Supervisorial District 8
- Historic status: **CEQA Category A — a historical resource is present**, on
  two grounds: the parcel falls within a California Register historic
  district, and a historic survey result applies

## Historic district

The parcel lies inside the **Castro & Liberty Streets Historic District**,
period of significance 1897–1906, found significant as a cohesive example of
pre-earthquake "streetcar suburb" residential development. The district is
**eligible** for the California Register — it is not listed on the National
Register, and it is not an Article 10 local landmark district, so the
protections of local landmark designation do not apply.
