# 746 Castro Street

An **1896** house identified as a historic resource in the city's planning
survey, held by the same ownership since 1989, and the subject of a $750,000
remodel application filed in May 2026 and still under review. Parcel
2752-017.

## The building

- Built 1896 (assessor roll)
- Single-family dwelling on the assessor roll, 7 rooms, 1 bath, 3 stories
  (but see "Open question" below)
- Building area 2,916 sq ft; lot 3,125 sq ft
- Wood-frame construction (assessor construction type D)
- Zoning RH-2; Supervisorial District 8
- Historic status: **CEQA Category A — a historical resource is present**,
  determined by historic survey. Not a designated city landmark; not within an
  Article 10 or 11 historic district (SF Planning, Historic Resource Status by
  Parcel)

## Open question — how many units?

The assessor roll records a single dwelling unit. Two recent permit
applications describe work at a "top unit" and relocating a "lower unit" to
the basement. The two city sources disagree, and this page does not resolve
the discrepancy.

## Ownership and value

The assessor's roll records the most recent sale on **March 10, 1989**. The
2025 assessed value is $469,109 (land) plus $466,291 (improvements) — $935,400
total.
