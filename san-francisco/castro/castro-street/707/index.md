# 707 Castro Street

A three-unit building of **1980** — by some eighty years the newest building
documented on the 700 block, and the only one built after the Second World
War. It stands inside the Castro & Liberty Streets Historic District. Parcel
3603-074.

## The building

- Built 1980 (assessor roll)
- Three residential units ("Apartment 4 units or less" property class),
  14 rooms, 5 baths, 2 stories
- Building area 3,441 sq ft; lot 2,625 sq ft
- Wood-frame construction (assessor construction type D)
- Zoning RH-3; Supervisorial District 8

## A modern building in a historic district

The parcel lies inside the **Castro & Liberty Streets Historic District**,
whose period of significance is 1897–1906 — so this building, completed in
1980, postdates the district by roughly three quarters of a century. It
nonetheless carries CEQA Category A status, which the Planning Department
assigns at parcel level to everything inside the district boundary. Whether
the building is classed as contributing or non-contributing to the district
is not established by the data used here.

The district is **eligible** for the California Register; it is not listed on
the National Register, and it is not an Article 10 local landmark district.

## Permit history

One permit is on file with the Department of Building Inspection — the
shortest record on the block.

- **2000** — reroofing ($13,875; expired) —
  [permit 200007245900](https://dbiweb02.sfgov.org/dbipts/default.aspx?page=Permit&PermitNumber=200007245900)

## Ownership and value

The 2025 assessed value is $24,480 (land) plus $385,333 (improvements) —
$409,813 total. The land figure is by far the lowest on the block, and unlike
every older building documented here the improvement value accounts for
almost the whole assessment. No sale date is recorded on the 2025 roll.
