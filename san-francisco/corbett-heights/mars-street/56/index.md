# 56 Mars Street

Among the earliest buildings documented on Mars Street, with a rear unit
numbered 56A.
Parcel 2654-009.

## Building facts

- Built: 1900 (assessor) — but the house appears in a photograph of about 1895
- Use: multi-family; 2 units; 11 rooms; 2 stories; 3 bathrooms
- Building area: 1,850 sq ft; lot 2,330 sq ft
- Construction: wood frame
- Rear unit: 56A Mars Street, addressed separately from 2010
- Zoning: RH-2
- Last recorded sale: 2012-10-03
- Historic status: CEQA Category C — assessed, not a historical resource

## Documented history

- **c. 1895** — standing in a photograph of Mars Street from Kite Hill
- **April 1906** — visible above the earthquake refugee camp at Corbett and Mars
- **1912** — photographed at close range with 52 and 64 Mars, already altered

## Permit history

- **2022-05** — horizontal addition of 670 sq ft above 56 and 56A, $367,835 (issued)
- **2010-07** — new foundations and floor slabs at the lower level, $75,000 (complete)
- **2010-01** — 56A assigned as the rear unit's legal address (issued)
- **2010-01** — two bathrooms and kitchen remodelled, $20,000 (complete)
- **2009-12** — three stair treads replaced, $200 (complete)
- **1990-12** — illegal kitchen removed, $2,500 (complete)

## Not yet documented

The builder and first owner, and whether the 2022 addition was built. The
assessor's 1900 date is later than the photograph of about 1895.
