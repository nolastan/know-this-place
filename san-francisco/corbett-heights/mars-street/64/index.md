# 64 Mars Street

A house built between the April 1906 earthquake and 1912, the year it first
appears in a photograph of the block. Parcel 2654-010.

## Building facts

- Built: 1908 (assessor); photographic evidence brackets it to 1906–1912
- Use: single-family dwelling; 5 rooms; 2 stories; 1 bathroom
- Building area: 1,206 sq ft; lot 2,469 sq ft
- Construction: wood frame
- Zoning: RH-2
- Last recorded sale: 2023-03-01
- Historic status: CEQA Category B — historic survey result, never formally evaluated
- Assessed 2025: land $1,092,420 + improvements $468,180 = $1,560,600

## Documented history

- **April 1906** — absent from the photograph of the earthquake refugee camp
- **1912** — present in a close photograph of the block with 52 and 56 Mars

## Permit history

- **2025-05** — third-floor bedroom and bathroom addition and new deck, $145,000 (issued)
- **2024-09** — eight windows replaced, $30,000 (complete)
- **2024-09** — reroofing, $26,000 (complete)
- **2024-09** — asbestos siding removed front and back, $6,000 (complete)
- **2023-03** — kitchen and bathroom remodelled in kind, $40,000 (issued)
- **1997-08** — garage walls strengthened and reroofed, $30,000 (complete)
- **1997-08** — demolition of the one-storey garage (filing)
- **1991-10** — deck removed, side roof resealed (complete)

## Not yet documented

The builder, architect and first owner, and the original building permit.
