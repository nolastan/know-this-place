# 52 Mars Street

Among the earliest buildings documented on Mars Street, greatly altered since
the 1920s.
Parcel 2654-008.

## Building facts

- Built: 1900 (assessor) — but the house appears in a photograph of about 1895
- Use: multi-family; 3 units; 10 rooms; 2 stories; 3 bathrooms
- Building area: 2,075 sq ft; lot 2,500 sq ft
- Construction: wood frame
- Zoning: RH-2
- Last recorded sale: 2021-04-26
- Historic status: CEQA Category B — historic survey result, never formally evaluated
- Assessed 2025: land $844,296 + improvements $562,864 = $1,407,160

## Documented history

- **c. 1895** — standing in a photograph of Mars Street from Kite Hill
- **April 1906** — visible above the earthquake refugee camp at Corbett and Mars
- **1912** — photographed at close range with 56 and 64 Mars
- **after 1921** — greatly altered; a photograph of that year still shows it unaltered

## Permit history

- **2022-11** — reroofing, $14,000 (complete)
- **2022-03** — dry rot damage repaired, $61,000 (complete)
- **2021-07** — plumbing and electrical replaced in kind at the rear, $12,000 (complete)
- **1997-08** — reroofing, $7,300 (complete)
- **1995-12** — reroofing, $5,250 (expired)
- **1992-02** — roof rafters and sheathing replaced, $16,000 (complete)

## Not yet documented

The builder and first owner, and which alterations were made after 1921. The
assessor's 1900 date is later than the photograph of about 1895.
