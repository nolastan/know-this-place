# 46–48 Mars Street

Among the earliest buildings documented on Mars Street, with a rear cottage on
the same parcel. Parcel 2654-007.

## Building facts

- Built: 1903 (assessor) — but the house appears in a photograph of about 1895
- Use: single-family dwelling with rear cottage; 8 rooms; 2 stories; 2 bathrooms
- Building area: 1,438 sq ft; lot 2,382 sq ft
- Construction: wood frame
- Street numbers: 46 (rear cottage) and 48 (front building), one parcel
- Zoning: RH-2
- Last recorded sale: 2005-04-29
- Historic status: CEQA Category B — never formally evaluated
- Assessed 2025: land $946,552 + improvements $631,033 = $1,577,585

## Documented history

- **c. 1895** — standing on the west side of Mars in a photograph from Kite Hill
- **April 1906** — visible above the earthquake refugee camp at Corbett and Mars
- **2005** — rear cottage renumbered from 48A to 46

## Permit history

- **2010-03** — front building compliance work, $10,000 (complete)
- **2010-02** — front stairs and railing repaired, $6,500 (complete)
- **2003-05** — interior remodel within the existing envelope, $120,000 (complete)
- **2000-10** — microfilm record corrected to show the rear cottage (complete)
- **1995-12** — dry rot and termite repair, $17,500 (complete)
- **1992-07** — front stairs rebuilt, $4,000 (complete)

## Not yet documented

The builder and first owner, and when the rear cottage was put up. The
assessor's 1903 construction date is later than the photograph of about 1895
in which the house already appears.
