# 60–62 Danvers Street

A two-flat building of 1961 at the north-west corner of Market and Danvers, on
ground that once carried part of a neighborhood grocery and saloon. Parcel
2659-060.

## Building facts

- Built: 1961
- Use: multi-family; 2 units; 10 rooms; 2 stories
- Building area: 2,732 sq ft; lot 2,652 sq ft
- Construction: wood frame
- Street numbers: 60 and 62 (one parcel)
- Zoning: RH-2
- Last recorded sale: 2002-09-27
- Historic status: CEQA Category B — never formally evaluated
- Assessed 2025: land $1,584,365 + improvements $758,378 = $2,342,743

## Documented history

- **before the Market Street extension** — part of Fred G. Horner's grocery and
  saloon, whose address was 2 Falcon Street, stood on this property

## Permit history

- **2015-09** — compliance with physical inspection report 7995, $20,000 (complete)
- **2011-05** — unit 62 kitchen remodel, $10,000 (complete)
- **2004-09** — unit 60 bathroom retiled, $2,800 (complete)
- **2002-12** — twenty windows replaced, $8,000 (complete)
- **1999-11** — siding and stucco repair, $3,200 (expired)
- **1996-06** — vinyl siding on rear and side walls, $5,300 (complete)
- **1986-11** — basement converted to bedroom and sewing room, $12,500 (expired)

## Not yet documented

What stood here between the removal of the grocery and the 1961 building, and
the permit for the present building. The assessor records no bathrooms, which
is unlikely to be right.
