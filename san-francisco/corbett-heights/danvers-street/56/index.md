# 56–58 Danvers Street

A two-unit building recorded as standing as built, photographed in 1909 beside
Falcon Street — a street the Market Street extension later erased. Parcel
2659-007.

## Building facts

- Built: 1900 (assessor)
- Use: multi-family; 2 units; 9 rooms; 2 stories; 4 bathrooms
- Building area: 2,589 sq ft; lot 1,873 sq ft
- Construction: wood frame
- Street numbers: 56 and 58 (one parcel)
- Zoning: RH-2
- Last recorded sale: 2017-06-27
- Historic status: CEQA Category B — never formally evaluated
- Assessed 2025: land $2,510,559 + improvements $1,075,952 = $3,586,511

## Documented history

- **April 1906** — probably the building at the right edge of the photograph of
  the earthquake refugee camp at Corbett and Mars
- **1909** — standing in a photograph of the 18th Street, Danvers and Falcon
  intersection, close to Fred G. Horner's grocery and saloon at 2 Falcon Street
- **1930s–1940s** — the Market Street extension is cut through the intersection,
  removing Falcon Street and the buildings around this one

## Permit history

- **2018-01** — final inspection for the 2013 work (complete)
- **2013-01** — kitchens and bathrooms remodelled with structural upgrades, $160,000 (complete)
- **2002-09** — front entry stairs repaired, $4,800 (complete)
- **1991-12** — reroofing, $12,500 (expired)

## Not yet documented

The builder and first owner, the original building permit behind the assessor's
1900 date, and the exact year the Market Street extension reached this block.
